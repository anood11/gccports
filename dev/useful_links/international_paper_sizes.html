International Paper Sizes (Trimmed)

The system of international paper sizes was devised by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO). The object of this system is to rationalize the range of paper sizes, which hitherto has been wide and unco-ordinated. Apart from the obvious advantages of an internationally-agreed system, the adoption of ISO sizes should bring rewards in efficiency and economy.

The principle on which ISO sizes are based is that the ratio between the longer and shorter sides of the sheet remains constant each time the sheet is cut or folded in half parallel to the shorter side. This is achieved by using a rectangle with the sides in the proportion of 1 : 2.

The basic size is that of the AO sheet, which has an area of 1 square metre ( m2). The other A sizes represent a halving at each stage of the previous sheet: the A1 sheet is half the AO and so on, with, in each case, the proportion between the and short sides being the same.

The 'A' series of paper sizes contains a range from A0 to A10. The 'B' series comprises sizes designated from B0 to B10 with the same proportions as the 'A' series, but giving intermediate sizes. It is emphasised that the feature of these series is that the dimensions are of the trimmed of finished sizes. The Post Office has detailed a range of preferred sizes of envelopes, with a similar ration of sides. Various 'A' and 'B' series dimensions, with equivalents are given in the adjoining.
